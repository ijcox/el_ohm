Schottky Diodes

Symbol: ➤| (low VF)

Overview

A Schottky diode is a semiconductor diode known for its low forward voltage drop and extremely fast switching speed.

Unlike conventional diodes, Schottky diodes are formed using a metal–semiconductor junction instead of a traditional p-n junction.

Construction

Schottky diodes are made by joining a metal (such as aluminum or platinum) with n-type semiconductor material, forming a Schottky barrier.

Because there is no p-type region, there is no charge storage effect like in standard p-n junction diodes.

Electrical Characteristics

  • Forward Voltage Drop: ~0.2V to 0.4V (lower than silicon diode)
  • Fast Switching Speed: Very low reverse recovery time
  • Higher Reverse Leakage Current: Compared to p-n diodes
  • Lower Reverse Voltage Ratings: Typically under 200V
P = V × I

Lower forward voltage reduces power loss in circuits, improving efficiency.

Standard Silicon Diode: ~0.7V Schottky Diode: ~0.3V

The reduced forward voltage makes Schottky diodes ideal for low-voltage applications.

Advantages

  • Low power dissipation
  • High efficiency
  • Very fast switching
  • Reduced heat generation

Disadvantages

  • Higher reverse leakage current
  • Lower maximum reverse voltage
  • More temperature-sensitive

Measurement and Testing

A Schottky diode can be tested using a digital multimeter in diode mode.

Forward voltage drop is measured in volts, and current ratings are specified in amperes.

Like other semiconductor devices, Schottky diodes are non-ohmic devices and do not follow a linear form of Ohm's Law.
