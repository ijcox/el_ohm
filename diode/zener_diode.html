Zener Diodes

Symbol: ➤|<

Overview

A Zener diode is a special type of diode designed to operate reliably in the reverse breakdown region. Unlike standard rectifier diodes, Zener diodes are intentionally used in reverse bias to regulate voltage.

They are widely used for voltage stabilization, reference generation, and circuit protection.

How It Works

When reverse voltage applied across a Zener diode reaches a specific value known as the Zener voltage (VZ), the diode begins conducting in reverse direction without damage.

Reverse Bias Region: Voltage ↑ | | Breakdown | ________ | / |______/____________ Vz

This controlled breakdown occurs due to the Zener effect (quantum tunneling) or avalanche breakdown, depending on the voltage rating.

Voltage Regulation Principle

Zener diodes maintain a nearly constant output voltage despite changes in input voltage or load current.

Vout ≈ VZ

A resistor is typically placed in series to limit current:

R = (Vin − VZ) / I

Although current is calculated using Ohm's Law, the diode itself is a non-ohmic device.

Key Electrical Characteristics

  • Zener Voltage (VZ): Rated breakdown voltage
  • Power Dissipation: Maximum power rating (P = V × I)
  • Knee Current: Minimum current for stable regulation
  • Dynamic Resistance: Small slope in breakdown region

Applications

  • Voltage regulation in power supplies
  • Over-voltage protection
  • Reference voltage generation
  • Waveform clipping circuits
  • Surge suppression

Testing and Measurement

Zener diodes can be tested using a digital multimeter in diode mode (forward direction) and with a regulated power supply to measure reverse breakdown voltage.

Measurements are expressed in volts for breakdown voltage and amperes for current ratings.

Zener vs. Standard Diodes

  • Standard diodes are not designed for reverse breakdown operation.
  • Zener diodes are optimized for stable reverse conduction.
  • Rectifier diodes focus on AC to DC conversion.
